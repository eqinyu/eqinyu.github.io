About me

I am a first-year doctoral student at the Graduate School of Interdisciplinary Information Studies in the University of Tokyo, Japan. I have a BA degree in advertising from East China Normal University, China, and a MA degree in Communication studies from Kent State University, USA. I believe that effective communication occurring between healthcare providers, policymakers, the media, and the public plays an important role in determining the quality of personal and public health. Therefore, my research focuses on examining media effects (including social media, entertainment media, and mobile Internet) on health risk beliefs from the psychological aspects. During my time at Kent, I led two research projects on health communication studies: (1) the first examined how Chinese online abortion ads promoted abortion services with emotional appeals, (2) the second examined news report framed the responsibility attribution of medical disputes in Chinese society. The research findings of these two studies have been published in 2016 and 2018 ICA annual conferences. My most recent project is developed around the topic of misinformation correction in social media. In this study, I attempt to understand what and how comments from other viewers provide individuals with deceptive clues of misinformation correcting news articles about Fukushima Nuclear accident.
